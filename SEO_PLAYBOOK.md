# SEO Playbook — Everyday Digital Solutions

A pragmatic, founder-operated SEO playbook for an AI & custom software studio
based in Mohali / Jalandhar serving Chandigarh, Mohali, Panchkula, and
Jalandhar. Goal: dominate **"app developer in Mohali"**, **"AI agency
Chandigarh"**, **"custom software Jalandhar"**, and locality long-tails inside
12–18 months without paid ads.

This playbook covers what the **founder** must do outside the codebase. The
codebase already handles the on-page work: schema, sitemap, prerender, locality
pages, calculators, and the lead capture engine.

---

## 1. Google Business Profile (GBP)

GBP is the single highest-leverage SEO asset for a local studio. Treat it like
a second homepage.

### Setup (one-time)

- Claim a profile for **Everyday Digital Solutions** at
  https://business.google.com.
- Use the founder's exact business name. **Do not** keyword-stuff
  (e.g. "Everyday Digital Solutions — App Developer Mohali" is a TOS violation
  and risks suspension).
- Address: use the registered business address. If you operate as a
  service-area business, hide the address and select Chandigarh, Mohali,
  Panchkula, Jalandhar as service areas (max 20 areas).
- Primary category: **Software company**.
- Secondary categories (add all that apply):
  - Mobile network operator → ❌ no
  - Website designer
  - Computer consultant
  - Marketing consultant
- Phone: WhatsApp Business number, international format.
- Website: `https://everydaydigitalsolutions.com`.
- Hours: actual hours. Add holiday hours (Diwali, Lohri, Holi, etc.).

### Weekly maintenance

- **Post weekly.** Use the "What's new" or "Offer" post type.
  - Mon: project highlight (anonymised — "Shipped a booking app for a
    Sector 8 salon this week").
  - Wed: educational post (link to a `/blog/*` post).
  - Fri: CTA post linking to `/tools/app-cost-calculator` or
    `/tools/ai-voice-agent-roi-calculator`.
- Add 1–2 new photos every week (office, team, screenshots of UI work,
  whiteboards). Geo-tag photos taken in Mohali/Chandigarh/Jalandhar where
  possible.
- Reply to every review within 24 hours.
- Answer every Q&A within 24 hours; pre-seed Q&A with 5 founder-asked
  questions ("Do you build apps for salons in Punjab?", etc.).

### Reviews

- Target: **2 new 5-star Google reviews per month**.
- Use `/admin/request-review` to send a personalised WhatsApp request to past
  clients. Send within 7 days of project handoff while the relationship is
  fresh.
- **Replace the placeholder** review URL in the request-review tool with the
  real share link from GBP → Get more reviews → Share review form.
- When a client gives a review, reply within 24 hours, mention the project
  type and city in the reply (e.g. "Thanks for the kind words on the Quasar
  Salon app — glad the Mohali team finds it useful.").
- Never offer money/discounts for reviews. It violates Google's terms.

Google trust your local presence. Quality > quantity. **Use the exact same
business name, address, and phone format everywhere.**

### Tier 2 — within 60 days

- GoodFirms.co — same as above.
- DesignRush — same.
- The Manifest
- AmbitionBox (employer brand → recruiting)
- Glassdoor (employer brand)
- AngelList / Wellfound (if pursuing tech talent)

### Tier 3 — local Punjab/Chandigarh

- Chandigarh business directories: `chandigarhbusinessguide.com`,
  `chdcity.com/directory`.

  Industries Association.

### Audit cadence

- Once per quarter, run a NAP audit: search the business name on Google and
  check that the top 20 results all show identical name/address/phone. Fix
  drift in the source listing.

## 3. Reviews Strategy

Reviews drive both ranking (proximity, prominence, relevance — the three GBP
ranking factors) and conversion (social proof on the locality pages).

### Volume target

| Quarter | Total Google reviews |
|---------|---------------------|
| Q1      | 6                   |
| Q2      | 12                  |
| Q3      | 20                  |
| Q4      | 30+                 |

30+ Google reviews puts EDS ahead of every other studio in the Tricity for
"app developer" searches by reviewer count alone.

### Process

1. Ship the project. Wait 7 days for the client to use it in production.
2. Founder sends a personal WhatsApp message via `/admin/request-review`.
3. If no review in 14 days, send one polite follow-up via WhatsApp.
4. If still none, drop it. Never nag — it damages the relationship.

### Diversification

- 80% Google. 10% Clutch. 10% LinkedIn recommendations.
- Ask one client per quarter for a video testimonial (1 min, phone shot is
  fine). Use these on `/work/*` pages and the homepage hero.

### Negative reviews

- Reply within 4 hours, professionally, with a path to resolution.
- Never argue. Never reveal client-specific details in public.
- Take it offline ("DM'd you on WhatsApp — let's get this fixed.").
- Document the root cause and update the playbook for future projects.

## 4. Backlinks

Backlinks are the slowest-moving SEO lever and the highest-impact one. Aim for
**1–2 quality dofollow links per month**. Quality = relevant, Indian or global
authority (DR 30+).

### Tactics that actually work for a Tricity studio

1. **Guest posts on Indian SaaS / startup blogs.** Write a 1500-word piece on
   "How a salon brand in Mohali moved 60% of bookings digital in 30 days" and
   pitch it to:
   - Inc42, YourStory (high DR, paid sponsored is OK if budget exists)
   - The Better India (story angle: small business digitisation)
   - LBB Chandigarh (lifestyle angle)
   - Tricity News, Chandigarh Metro
2. **Open source one tool.** Publish `app-cost-calculator` logic as an
   MIT-licensed npm package. Submit to awesome-india lists on GitHub.
3. **Conference talks.** Apply to speak at:
   - JS Foo (Reactify), India FOSS, Rootconf
   - Local: Chandigarh Angels Network meetups, TiE Chandigarh
4. **Founder LinkedIn.** Post 3x/week. Tag clients (with permission).
   LinkedIn → website is a strong indirect signal.
5. **Punjab University & Thapar engineering departments.** Sponsor a small
   hackathon prize (₹25k) for a `.edu.in` link from the event page.
6. **Reciprocal with non-competing local agencies.** A "tools we love" page
   exchange with a Mohali design studio is fine; with another dev studio is
   not.

### Anchor-text policy

- 60% branded ("Everyday Digital Solutions", "EDS", "everydaydigitalsolutions.com").
- 30% generic ("this article", "case study", "their team").
- 10% partial-match ("Mohali app developer EDS", "AI voice agent studio").
- **Never** exact-match anchor more than 1 in 50 links. Triggers Penguin.

### What NOT to do

- ❌ Buy backlinks from Fiverr / private blog networks.
- ❌ Comment-spam on blogs.
- ❌ Submit to free directories with DR < 20.
- ❌ Reciprocal link exchanges with unrelated sites.

## 5. Content Calendar

A sustainable cadence the founder can keep up alongside billable work.

### Weekly cadence

| Day | Channel | Output |
|-----|---------|--------|
| Mon | LinkedIn | Project highlight (anonymised), 150 words |
| Tue | GBP post | "What's new" with link to a service page |
| Wed | Blog | New `/blog/*` post (1200–1800 words, 1 per week) |
| Thu | Instagram + GBP photo | Behind-the-scenes shot of work |
| Fri | LinkedIn | Educational post (link to the new blog post) |

### Monthly cadence

- 1 long-form case study (1 per project shipped → `/work/*` page).
- 1 founder appearance (podcast, panel, guest post, university talk).
- 1 GBP Q&A new question seeded.
- 1 new locality page if expanding service area (currently 8 — see
  `src/content/locality-pages.ts`).

### Quarterly cadence

- 1 free tool launched (Q1 done: app cost + ROI calculator. Q2 candidate:
  "WhatsApp Business API ROI calculator". Q3: "AI receptionist script
  generator". Q4: "Custom CRM vs SaaS calculator").
- 1 lead-magnet PDF (Q1: app cost guide 2026. Q2: AI voice agent buyer's
  guide. Q3: WhatsApp automation playbook for clinics. Q4: year-end review).
- 1 backlink campaign (one of the Tactics from §4).
- NAP audit across all citations.

### Editorial focus

Write for the **owner of a service business in Punjab** — not for other
developers. Voice: confident, specific, allergic to fluff. Always cite a
₹ figure or a percentage, never round prose.

#### Topic priorities (in order)

1. **City + service** ("App developer in Mohali", "AI agency Chandigarh",
   "Custom software Jalandhar"). One post per locality per quarter.
2. **Industry + outcome** ("How clinics in Sector 22 cut no-shows with
   WhatsApp reminders").
3. **Buyer education** ("How much does an app cost in India in 2026", "AI
   voice agent vs human receptionist: when each wins").
4. **Behind the scenes** ("How we shipped Quasar in 30 days").

### Distribution

Every blog post must be:

1. Published with full schema (handled by `SEO.tsx` + `BreadcrumbsJsonLd`).
2. Posted to GBP "What's new" within 24 hours.
3. Posted as a LinkedIn carousel within 48 hours.
4. Sent to the email list (when the list reaches 100+ — for now, just save
   subscribers via `/api/subscribers`).
